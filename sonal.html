sonal: ambient awareness

tu/e design project | 2021

An audio-based awareness system, designed to strengthen social presence and family connection in a remote work environment.

Working from home is rarely a new phenomenon. However, being away from the social environment of the workplace can cause isolation among home workers. Additionally, when work encroaches on the domestic sphere, the identity conflicts of workers and partners/parents bring about psychosocial health problems.

Sonal explores intimacy and family connection through audio, by capturing sounds from around the house to create a remote awareness system within the home office. Mundane sounds of ongoing events are transformed into a musical soundscape in the home office, with the user being able to tune the soundscape from abstract to clear depending on their work desires and concentration level. With the impression of ambient sound display, home workers can be aware of what other family members are doing.

For this project, a sound manipulation system and control interface was designed, which were subsequently evaluated in a real work environment, with mundane sounds that are personal and familiar to the participants. While the study was mainly exploratory, the results showed that the system can potentially contribute to an increased connection with the family, while not interfering with the home office environment.

In collaboration with Bangyue Li and Tianqin Lu

keywords: sound design, calm technology, peripheral awareness, generative audio
